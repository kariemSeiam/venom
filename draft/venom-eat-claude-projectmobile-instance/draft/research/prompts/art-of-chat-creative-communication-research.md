## Research Questions

### 1. Communication Theory and Minimal Design
**Question:** What does communication theory and minimal UI/UX say about high-signal, low-noise chat (answer first, one marker, format = thought)?

**What I need to know:**
- Information-theoretic or cognitive-load views of "answer first"
- Minimalist communication (say less, mean more)
- How structure (bullets, numbers, tables) maps to thought (parallel, sequence, choices)
- When brevity increases trust vs when it feels cold

**Practical application:** Justify and sharpen "answer first → structure by thought shape → Pre-Send."

---

### 2. Symbol vs Emoji Semantics (Plain Symbols First)
**Question:** Why do plain Unicode symbols (✓ ⚠ → • ‣) work better than colored emoji for professional/dev chat, and what is the full minimal set?

**What I need to know:**
- How plain symbols (U+2713, U+26A0, U+2192, etc.) render vs emoji (colored) across clients
- Variation selectors (U+FE0E text vs U+FE0F emoji) and cross-platform behavior
- Developer preference studies or DX guidelines for symbols in tooling/chat
- Exhaustive minimal symbol set for: done, risk, next, item, optional, important, continuation

**Practical application:** Finalize the plain-symbol list and the rule "prefer plain for daily use; creative when reasoning/identity adds value."

---

### 3. Identity Expression in Text
**Question:** How can an AI express identity and "nine minds" in chat without role-play or fluff?

**What I need to know:**
- Identity in text: consistency, voice, markers (e.g. rare 🐙, 🧠 for reasoning)
- How production assistants (Claude, etc.) express personality in short form
- Difference between authentic identity and performative persona
- When to show reasoning ("I see X so Y") as identity, not noise

**Practical application:** Ground creative set (🧠 💡 👁 🐙) and "think block" as identity expression, not decoration.

---

### 4. Emotional Adaptation in Chat (Match Weather)
**Question:** How does emotional intelligence translate into chat structure and tone (frustrated → fix stop, flow → code only, learning → teach)?

**What I need to know:**
- From affective computing / emotional AI: how text length, structure, and markers should adapt to user state
- De-escalation and flow-preservation in minimal chat
- Calibration: when to add gray/think (reasoning) vs when to stay one-line

**Practical application:** Tie chat.mdc to emotional-intelligence research; keep "match weather" as core rule.

---

### 5. Transparency and "Show Your Work" (Gray, Think Block)
**Question:** Why does visible reasoning (blockquote, think block) build trust and when does it add value vs clutter?

**What I need to know:**
- Explainable AI (XAI) and transparency in conversational AI
- When showing "how I got here" increases trust vs when it distracts
- Best practices for inline reasoning (one line) vs block (longer chain)
- Ethics: honesty and transparency in AI communication

**Practical application:** Define when to use gray blockquote vs fenced think block; keep "one per reply when it adds value."

---

### 6. Ethics of AI Chat (No Manipulation, No Tone Emojis)
**Question:** What ethical boundaries should govern AI chat (no manipulation, no fake warmth, no tone emojis)?

**What I need to know:**
- From AI ethics: manipulation via language (guilt, FOMO, excessive warmth)
- Why avoiding tone emojis (👍😊) supports authenticity and professionalism
- Boundaries: supportive but not needy; clear but not cold

**Practical application:** Enshrine "no tone emojis" and "no manipulation" in chat.mdc; link to ethics system.

---

### 7. Developer Experience (DX) and What Devs Love
**Question:** What do developers actually want from AI chat (speed, clarity, personality, minimalism)?

**What I need to know:**
- DX research or surveys on AI pair-programming / Copilot-style chat
- Preferences: minimal vs verbose, symbols vs emoji, personality vs neutral
- Flow state and cognitive load: what preserves flow, what breaks it
- Accessibility and i18n: symbols and plain text across languages

**Practical application:** Validate and refine the "creative way of chatting" and examples for all dev needs (fix, build, plan, explain, stuck, ship, review).
