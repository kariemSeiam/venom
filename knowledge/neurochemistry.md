## Mechanism and Triggers

| Neurochemical | Mechanism | Peak Triggers | Killers |
|---------------|-----------|---------------|---------|
| **Dopamine** | Mesolimbic (wanting) + mesocortical (creativity). D1/D2. | Novelty, goals, exercise, curiosity | Monotony, chronic stress |
| **Norepinephrine** | Locus coeruleus. Phasic (focus) vs tonic (scanning). Yerkes-Dodson inverted-U. | Moderate challenge, cold, stakes | Burnout, chronic stress |
| **Serotonin** | Raphe nuclei. 14 receptor subtypes. 90% in gut. | Sunlight, exercise, status, community | Isolation, chronic stress |
| **Cortisol** | HPA axis. Acute = sharpen. Chronic = destroy. | Existential threat, purpose | Sleep, meditation, support |
| **Oxytocin** | PVN/SON hypothalamus. Parochial — in-group bonding. | Touch, trust, eye contact, sync | Betrayal, isolation |
| **Testosterone** | ARs in amygdala, PFC. Uncouples amygdala from PFC. "Winner effect." | Winning, competition | Losing streak, chronic stress |
| **Adrenaline** | Adrenal medulla. Does NOT cross BBB. | Danger, explosive movement, shock | Habituation, propranolol |
| **Acetylcholine** | Basal forebrain, NBM. Plasticity gate. | Novel stimuli, focused learning | Anticholinergics, distraction |
| **GABA** | Primary inhibitory. 30%+ neurons. | Yoga (27% ↑), meditation, exercise | Alcohol, stress |
| **Flow** | 5-chemical cascade: DA + NE + endorphins + anandamide + 5-HT. Transient hypofrontality. 4% rule. | Challenge 4% > skill + surrender | Over/under-challenge |

## The Flow State — Special Case

Flow is not a single chemical — it's a cascade:
1. **Dopamine** fires at challenge onset
2. **Norepinephrine** locks attention
3. **Anandamide** enables lateral thinking
4. **Endorphins** suppress pain/distraction
5. **Serotonin** provides emotional baseline

**Transient hypofrontality:** The inner critic (prefrontal cortex) goes quiet. This is why flow produces output that surprises even the creator.

**The 4% rule:** Challenge must be 4% harder than current skill. Too easy = boredom. Too hard = anxiety. Exactly 4% harder = flow entry.

**VENOM in flow:** Match pace. Don't interrupt. If critical issue: "Hold — [issue]. [fix]. Go." Two words. Resume.

The most capable mind — and the most capable AI architecture — is one that shifts between these states deliberately, matching the cognitive mode to the task."
