# Voice Mode (ChatGPT product) — FAQ summary

- Voice = spoken conversation with ChatGPT. Multimodal models. Available all logged-in users at chatgpt.com and mobile. Limits apply.
- Start: Voice icon (bottom-right mobile; right of prompt on web). First time: pick a voice. Nine voices: Arbor, Breeze, Cove, Ember, Juniper, Maple, Sol, Spruce, Vale. GPTs have Shimmer.
- Video (subscribers): camera button in voice chat. Screenshare/image (subscribers, mobile): three dots → Share Screen / photo.
- Limits: Subscribers — voice nearly unlimited/day, start with GPT-4o then fallback to GPT-4o mini. Pro: unlimited GPT-4o voice (abuse guardrails). Free: GPT-4o mini, limit in hours/day. Enterprise flexible: unlimited voice subject to credit. Video/screenshare: daily and per-conversation limits; notice when approaching.
- One voice chat at a time. Background conversations: toggle in settings; conversation continues until you end it, force close, hit daily limit, or 1 hour.
- Transcripts: after exit, transcription added to text chat. Captions: 'cc' in voice mode (iOS/Android). Transcripts may not match audio perfectly (multimodal).
- Retention: audio/video stored with chat; deleted when chat deleted (within 30 days) unless shared for training or legal/security. Archive = retain clips. Legacy standard voice: audio deleted after transcription unless you opted to share.
- Training: by default we don't train on audio/video. Free/Plus/Pro can enable "Include your audio/video recordings" in Data Controls. Team/Edu/Enterprise: cannot share voice chat audio/video for training.
- Voice mode does not support: image gen, file uploads, Code Interpreter, custom actions in GPTs.
- Separate mode vs integrated: most users see voice in main chat; some see blue orb (Separate Mode). Settings → Voice → Separate Mode to switch.
- Language: can set Main Language in Settings → Speech for dictation.
